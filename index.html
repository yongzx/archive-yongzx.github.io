I am a second-year PhD student in the Department of Computer Science, Brown University under the advisorship of Prof. Stephen Bach. I am primarily interested in low-resource NLP, and I've explored topics such as zero-shot prompting, weak supervision, and multilingual NLP.

Prior to my PhD, I completed my BSc at Minerva University where I had the opportunity to study/live in multiple countries such as Argentina, India, Germany, South Korea, U.K., and USA.

Projects

BigScience: The Summer of Language Models (2021 - 2022)
The BigScience effort is an excited international collaboration aimed at training a very large and open language model for the research community. I led the language adaptation research (BLOOM+1) in the multilingual modeling subgroup, and I helped out in the prompt engineering subgroup (T0, PromptSource, and BLOOMZ).
